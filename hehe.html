DATA ANALYTICS AND BUSSINESS INTELLIGENCE

EDIFYBIZ SHOW

01

EdifyBiz Show is a custom solution for all of your company’s data analytics and BI needs. It is capable of creating simplified infographics of the analysed data and key performance areas required from the management standpoint. A dashboard that summarises your business exactly the way you want and is available on any device.

TABLEAU

02

Tableau is presently the most popular and widely used BI product. A sophisticated data finding and exploration tool that helps you to quickly find answers to critical problems. It features the best visualisation capabilities and a flawless graphical user interface on the front end. It also has built-in analytics modules that the user may operate directly on their data.

It allows data from a variety of sources to be combined. It can store data in memory or retrieve it directly from the source if the files are too vast to accommodate in memory. It allows users to configure dynamic visuals, create app objects, and share reports and documents with other users via the internet.

MICROSOFT POWER BI

03

Microsoft’s Power BI is a business analytics tool that allows you to visualise data and share insights throughout your organisation, as well as integrate them in your app or website. Your data could be in the form of an Excel spreadsheet or a collection of hybrid data warehouses that are both cloud-based and on-premises. Power BI makes it simple to connect to your data sources, display what’s essential, and find out what’s significant.

For users who are familiar with Excel, Power BI’s user interface is relatively intuitive, and its strong connectivity with other Microsoft products makes it an extremely adaptable self-service tool with little upfront training.

GOOGLE DATA STUDIO

04

Data Studio, also known as Google Data Studio, is a reporting and data visualisation application developed by Google. It gathers data from up to 12 various sources, including Google Analytics, and compiles it into a report that is simple to edit, distribute, and read. Widgets, graphs, charts, and maps are also available to help you visualise your data. Data Studio reports can be broken down into one page or twenty, illustrating the flexibility and customisation of Google Data Studio. It adjusts to your company’s specific requirements, making data sharing a stress-free experience. Data Studio is essentially a geared up version of Google Analytics dashboard, which is notoriously limited in terms of features.
